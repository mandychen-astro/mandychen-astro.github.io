Research

Research

My current research focuses on observations of the circumgalactic medium (CGM), the dynamical state of the CGM and its connection to the star-formation/AGN activities of galaxies and the cosmic baryon cycle in general. I also have extensive experience in the modeling of strong gravitational lensing clusters. My PhD advisor is Prof. Hsiao-Wen Chen.

Below you can find a list of my recent papers.

Resolved galactic superwinds reconstructed around their host galaxies at z > 3
Chen et al. 2021, MNRAS. In this paper we presented a detailed analysis of two giant Lyman-alpha arcs at z > 3 strongly lensed by a massive cluster. Using spatially-resolved line profiles revealed in deep MUSE observations, we identified these arcs as supergalactic winds driven by nearby star-forming galaxies.
